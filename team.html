Dr. Karun Kaniyamattam

Dr. Kaniyamattam leads our laboratory's mission to develop artificial intelligence-based decision models for sustainable livestock systems. His expertise spans mathematical modeling, epidemiology, and economic systems analysis.

Adeolu Joseph Adekunle, DVM, EMBA

Doctoral Researcher – Artificial Intelligence for Sustainable Livestock Systems,

Adeolu is an interdisciplinary researcher and innovator at Texas A&M University, advancing the science and application of AI-driven decision modeling for sustainable livestock systems. His work sits at the intersection of epidemiology, socio-economics, and precision livestock technology, with a focus on livestock diseases of public health importance such as Bovine respiratory disease (BRD).

As a member of the Artificial Intelligence for Sustainable Livestock Systems Lab, Adeolu designs and deploys machine learning algorithms, system dynamics models, and agent-based simulations to predict, manage, and economically evaluate disease control strategies. He has pioneered and contributed to integrated solutions, including a feedlot economic decision visualization tool, a computer vision pipeline for cattle identification and BRD prediction, a system dynamics model of BRD in US feedlot operations, and a veterinary syndromic surveillance solution spanning multiple U.S. states. His research directly supports antimicrobial stewardship, producer profitability, and One Health goals.

With over a decade of experience bridging academia, government, and industry, Adeolu has led and contributed to several multi-million-dollar international development projects, national policy frameworks, and agtech product development. A veterinarian with an executive MBA, Adeolu also mentors emerging scientists through the Aggie Research Program (now Aggie Collaborate), global outreaches, STEM leadership initiatives, and the TAMU Graduate Mentoring Academy. His work exemplifies translational research that transforms data into actionable insight for a resilient, profitable, and health-secure livestock system.

Serinmary Pulikkottil

Doctoral Researcher - Sustainable Livestock Production Systems

Serin is a Ph.D. researcher in Animal Science at Texas A&M University, where she uses modeling to improve the sustainability of livestock production systems. Her work integrates artificial intelligence, system dynamics, and economic analysis to evaluate strategies for reducing methane emissions and enhancing production efficiency.

By leveraging data from precision livestock technologies, she supports informed decision-making in beef systems. Serin is also passionate about mentoring students and contributing to science-based agricultural policy.

Vishnudas Kulangara Veettil

Doctoral Researcher - Agent-Based Modeling, Machine Learning for Beef Value Chain

Vishnu is a Ph.D. student in the Artificial Intelligence for Sustainable Livestock Systems lab at Texas A&M University. He holds a BVSc & AH from Kerala Veterinary and Animal Sciences University, an MVSc in Livestock Economics from ICAR–Indian Veterinary Research Institute, and two postgraduate diplomas in Small Animal Theriogenology and Infectious Disease Management. His research combines system dynamics, agent-based modeling, and machine learning to study economic and biological interactions in the U.S. beef value chain. His work focuses on modeling profitability, market dynamics, and animal health impacts, and developing blockchain-enabled traceability and digital twin frameworks for decision support.

Aparna Pushkaran Ajitha

Doctoral Researcher - Epidemiological Modeling, Animal Science

Aparna is a doctoral researcher in the Department of Animal Science at Texas A&M University, working under the guidance of Dr. Karun Kaniyamattam. My research focuses on resource efficiency in dairy production systems, with a particular emphasis on analyzing the interlinked use of water, energy, and feed in modern dairy farms. By applying systems modeling and decision science approaches, my work aims to improve the sustainability, resilience, and productivity of livestock systems. Prior to starting my Ph.D., I worked on the development of a system dynamics model for Foot-and-Mouth Disease (FMD). This project combined epidemiological modeling and simulation to better understand disease transmission pathways and to evaluate intervention strategies at the herd and population levels. This experience fostered my interest in integrating animal health, systems modeling, and sustainability into livestock research. My broader research interests include: Systems dynamics modeling for livestock health and production, Sustainable resource management in dairy and beef systems, Animal disease modeling with a focus on prevention and control strategies, and Decision-support frameworks for livestock producers to enhance resilience under environmental and economic constraints.

Through this work, I aim to contribute to the development of data-driven, sustainable solutions that address the challenges of animal health, productivity, and resource management in the livestock sector.

Dr. Megha Poyyara Saiju

Researcher - Nutritional Physiology and Functional Food Production

Dr. Megha Poyyara Saiju (DVM, MVSc, PhD, PGTMD) is a veterinary science researcher specialised in nutritional physiology and functional food production. Her research work explored the estrogenic impact of soybean isoflavones on ovarian receptor dynamics in layer chickens. She also pioneered a safe and effective method to produce vitamin D3- and zinc-enriched designer eggs. She has over five years of experience in analytical and molecular techniques from a NABL-accredited Central Instruments Laboratory, and is currently contributing to multidisciplinary research initiatives at the Artificial Intelligence for Sustainable Livestock Systems Lab at Texas A&M University
